Nasi Lemak Tutorial

Description

Nasi Lemak is a beloved Malaysian dish that translates to "rich rice," referring to the creamy, aromatic rice cooked in coconut milk and pandan leaves. This dish is often considered Malaysia's national dish and is enjoyed at any time of the day, whether as breakfast, lunch, or dinner. Traditionally served with a variety of accompaniments, Nasi Lemak includes sambal (a spicy chili paste), crispy fried anchovies (ikan bilis), roasted peanuts, hard-boiled eggs, and fresh cucumber slices. For a more filling meal, it can be paired with side dishes like fried chicken, curry, or rendang. Its combination of sweet, spicy, and savory flavors makes it a true delight for the taste buds.

Lists of Ingredients

    For the Coconut Rice:

  • 2 cups of rice (preferably long-grain or jasmine rice)
  • 1 cup of coconut milk
  • 2-3 pandan leaves (knotted)
  • 2 cups of water (adjust as needed based on your rice type)
  • 1 teaspoon of salt

    • For the Sambal (Spicy Chili Paste):

  • 2 tablespoons of oil
  • 1 large onion (finely sliced)
  • 3-4 dried chilies (soaked and blended)
  • 2 fresh red chilies (blended)
  • 2 cloves of garlic (minced)
  • 1 tablespoon of tamarind paste (mixed with 1/4 cup water)
  • 1 teaspoon of sugar (adjust to taste)
  • Salt (to taste)

    • For the Sides:

  • 1 cup of ikan bilis (fried anchovies)
  • 1/2 cup of roasted peanuts
  • 2 hard-boiled eggs (halved)
  • 1 cucumber (sliced)
  • Optional: fried chicken, rendang, or curry

Steps on how to make it

    Step 1: Cook the Coconut Rice

  1. Rinse the rice thoroughly to remove excess starch.
  2. In a rice cooker or pot, combine the rinsed rice, coconut milk, pandan leaves, water, and salt.
  3. Cook until the rice is fluffy and aromatic. If using a pot, bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer until cooked.

    4. Step 2: Prepare the Sambal

  4. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat.
  5. Saut√© the sliced onions, blended chilies, and minced garlic until fragrant.
  6. Add the tamarind water, sugar, and salt, then stir to combine.
  7. Simmer the mixture on low heat for about 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sambal thickens. Adjust sweetness and spiciness to taste.

    8. 3: Fry the Anchovies

  8. Heat oil in a small pan.
  9. Fry the ikan bilis (anchovies) until crispy. Remove and set aside.

    10. Step 4: Prepare the Sides

  10. Roast the peanuts in a dry pan or bake them until golden brown.
  11. Slice the cucumber into thin rounds.
  12. Boil the eggs, peel, and cut them in half.
  13. Optionally, prepare fried chicken, curry, or rendang as additional sides.
